02The loan product

Get A Cash Advance Today underwrites fixed-rate, unsecured personal installment loans with simple-interest amortization, monthly payment frequency, and no balloon, no insurance bundling, no add-on fees, and no prepayment penalty. Principal ranges from $500 to $15,000; term ranges from 3 to 60 months; APR ranges from 5.99% to 35.99% inclusive of all finance charges.

The loan is a closed-end credit product within the meaning of Regulation Z § 1026.2(a)(10): the disbursed principal is fixed at the outset, the repayment schedule is fixed at the outset, and the contract terminates upon receipt of the final scheduled payment or sooner upon prepayment in full.

03APR, finance charge, monthly payment

Your annual percentage rate is determined at underwriting using the rate-tier matrix published on our home page; the four tiers are Excellent (740+ FICO — 5.99% APR), Good (680–739 — 14.99%), Fair (620–679 — 24.99%) and Building (below 620 — 35.99%).

The monthly payment M is computed using the standard amortization formula M = P · r / (1 − (1 + r)−n), where P is the principal, r is the periodic rate (APR ÷ 12 ÷ 100), and n is the term in months. The total of payments is M · n; the finance charge is the total of payments minus the principal. These three figures, alongside the APR and the payment-due dates, are restated in the Truth-in-Lending box of the loan agreement before you sign it.

08Repayment schedule

Each loan is repaid in n equal monthly installments, where n is the term in months you selected at the application. Installments are due on the same calendar date each month, beginning thirty days after the funding date. The amortization schedule annexed to your loan agreement lists the specific calendar dates, the principal and interest split of each installment, and the running balance after each payment.

09Late charges & default

An installment is considered late if not paid in full by the close of business on the calendar day ten days after its due date. A flat $15 late charge accrues on each late installment. We will waive the late charge on a borrower's first occurrence of lateness if you contact support before the due date and arrange a deferral or partial-payment plan in writing.

An installment that remains unpaid 60 days after its due date constitutes an event of default, at which point the entire remaining principal balance, accrued interest and accrued late charges become immediately due and payable, and the loan is reported as charged off to the consumer-reporting agencies in line with industry-standard furnisher practice.

10Prepayment

You may prepay any portion or the entire outstanding balance of your loan, at any time, without penalty. Interest is accrued on a simple-interest, daily-pro-rata basis on the outstanding principal balance, so a prepayment in excess of the scheduled installment will save you the unaccrued interest on the prepaid principal for the remainder of the loan term.
